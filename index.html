About Us

Regan has been involved in teaching and editing for over 30 years. She started her career in Japan teaching English to businessmen and editing English textbooks targeted at teaching businessmen. She later moved to France where she wrote for Boulevard magazine and worked as a freelance translator from French to English. More recently, she has donated her work as a tutor, counselor and editor to underserved minority communities in East Harlem, NY.

The college application process can be overwhelming -- testing, essays, recommendations, early admissions, early action, binding, non-binding. It can feel like a foreign language within a foreign language.
We are here to decode it for you. We are here to virtually hold your hand through the process and most importantly to guide your student to the best application they can put forward. From choosing a` la carte for help with a single essay to working with our staff to determine school choice, test timing and completion of all your college applications, we are here to help your student transition from high school to university with the greatest success possible.
Through a combination of on-line meetings (Skype, Google Chat, FaceTime, WhatsApp) and document exchanges we will assist your student in organizing their thoughts to whittle down and settle on an essay topic. From there we will work with them as they craft and shape an essay that reflects their unique voice and passion.
One of the trickiest parts of creating that perfect essay is saying what you want to say in the precise number of characters or words allotted. We will help you cut or add to your essays until they are exactly the right length.
For many families deciphering the various applications (financial aid, TOEFL, SAT, ACT, etc) can be mind-numbing. We can take that job off your plate to give you and your student the opportunity to focus on the critical tasks of test preparation and essay writing.

Wills and Won'ts

Wills

  • We will create a structure to guide your student in writing and rewriting their personal essays.
  • We will meet with your student virtually to craft and discuss their plan of action, brainstorm ideas for their essays, and get them started.
    • Edits will include proofreading and critiquing structure and content, as well as assistance with word choice and voice
  • We will continue with a back and forth of documents until the student feels the essays are ready or it’s time to submit them.
    • We commit to turning around your student’s work in 48 hours.
    • We want your student to be happy with their essay and we are committed to working with them as much as they would like until it is time to submit them. *
  • We can assist with all the essays on the application. According to college admissions officers, the short-answer essays can be even more important than the personal essay. Don’t dash them off, take the time to write a thoughtful response and have it professionally edited as you did with the personal essay.
  • We can assist with filling out all application forms:
    • SAT, ACT, TOEFEL, SAT2
    • FAFSA (for financial aid)
    • CSS Profile (for financial aid)
    • We can assist parents with drafting a concise and articulate answer to “family circumstances” if there are special financial circumstances you need to convey to the CSS group.
  • We will help create an optimal plan of action for the timing of testing and writing of applications.
  • We are happy to discuss the completeness and complexity of your student’s list of potential colleges and their various application deadlines (early decision, early decision II, early action, rolling admissions) as a compliment to their high school college counselors.
      • These are big decisions. Some students like to hear more than one voice and opinion before making final decisions on where to apply.

Won'ts

  • We will not write your students’ essays.
      • Our goal is the ultimate success of your student. We want to see your student in the best college or university that fits their profile. Setting them up for success requires that they be prepared for the rigors of their chosen school and confident in their ability to succeed. That starts with first and foremost their high school transcript. It continues with their standardized testing and culminates in their personal essays and applications.
  • We will reach out to the student if we haven’t heard from them at the appointed time and we will then reach out to you, the parents. But we can-not make your students do the work.
  • This is not a translation service. We want your student to be prepared for the university experience and being able to work in English is a big part of that.
*In the interest of keeping the students calm, the deadlines will always be ten days before the actual school deadlines. (For example, if University of Florida has a December 31 deadline to apply, The Essay Fixer deadline will be December 21).
